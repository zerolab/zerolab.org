> **NOTE** This is my living checklist on hackathons

## For organisers

0. Keep messaging consistent <br/>
   <small>Especially around timing. Be clear on any overheads and potential delays (e.g. sign-in, identity check, chaperone/escort)</small>
1. Compile and share relevant resources ahead of time <br/>
   <small>This is especially relevant if there is scope to work with large datasets.</small>
2. Limit non-productive time <br/>
   <small>Or, better said, balance background and context giving with time for doing.</small>
3. Consider a separate preparatory session <br/> 
   <small>If the focus of the event is highly specialised, consider holding a prep session in-person or online ahead of the event.</small>
4. Ensure facilitators are always there <br/>
   <small>If you provide facilitators, ensure they are reachable and engaged. It is also important to keep an eye on folks that are less engaged, and help</small>
6. Provide a steady supply of coffee <br/>
   <small>Having structured breaks is great, however a steady supply of warm/cold beverages goes a long way to fuel the participants without distracting from the task at hand</small>

## For participants

1. Prepare ahead of time <br/>
   <small>Download any large datasets, check additional tooling and resources shared by organisers.</small>
2. When joining as group, align and test your development environments and tooling
   <small>This will ensure you don't suprises during the event, and can work together effectively.</small>
3. Bring extra paraphernalia, but not too much <br/>
   <small>Bring your charger, cables, adapters (e.g. <abbr title="High-Definition Multimedia Interface">HDMI</abbr>), a flash drive in case you need to move data quickly</small>
4. Set a deadline for deciding <br/>
   <small>Oftentimes there will be multiple ideas and avenues to pursue. It is better to try 2-3 of them, than take all the time to settle on one thing.</small>
5. Make space for smaller teams if needed<br/>
   <small><abbr title="In My Humble Opinion">IMHO</abbr>, a good size is 4-5 people.</small>
6. Designate a leader, and listen to them<br/>
   <small>If everyone leads, no one is.</small>
7. Leave time to present <br/>
   <small>Sharing and presenting off-the-cuff doesn't come easy for most people.</small>
8. Don't forget to have fun! <br/>
   <small>It is easy to get lost in the task or become too competitive. Make sure you're enjoying the process, and others do too.</small>
